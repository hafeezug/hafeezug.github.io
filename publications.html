Publications

Publications in reverse chronological order. My work centers on NLP for the Balochi language and multilingual evaluation of large language models.

2026
Global PIQA: Evaluating Physical Commonsense Reasoning Across 100+ Languages and Cultures Submitted to NeurIPS
Tyler A. Chang, et al. — including Hafeez Ullah (co-author, Balochi language)
arXiv preprint arXiv:2510.24081
A massive participatory commonsense-reasoning benchmark built by 350+ researchers from 65+ countries, covering 141 language varieties across five continents, 19 language families, and 24 writing systems. I verified translations and integrated culturally specific elements for the Balochi language, helping assess cross-lingual performance gaps — up to 68% accuracy difference between languages — in state-of-the-art LLMs.
arXiv PDF
2025
Balochi Named Entity Recognition Using Machine Learning Approach In preparation
Hafeez Ullah
Ready for publication
Introduces BNer, the first gold-standard Named Entity Recognition dataset for the Balochi language — 58,893 tokens across five domains. Evaluates CRFs, SVMs, and KNNs; SVM achieves an optimal 96% F1-score by leveraging POS features and the BILOU tagging scheme.
2025
Word Embedding for Arabic Script Languages — A Survey In preparation
Hafeez Ullah
Ready for publication
A comprehensive systematic review of 100+ research papers (2013–2025) tracking the evolution of word embedding techniques — from static embeddings to large language models — across Arabic-script languages. Identifies critical resource gaps for understudied languages like Balochi and outlines future directions for a unified NLP ecosystem.
